Problem 28.03 Helical motion of a charged particle in a magnetic field
An electron with a kinetic energy of 22 eV moves into a region of uniform magnetic field of magnitude T. The angle between the direstions of and the electron's velocity is . What is the pitch of the helical path taken by the electron?
Variables
m = 9.1e-31; % kg mass of an electron
e = 1.6e-19; % C elementary charge
q = -e;
E_k = 22; % eV kinetic energy
B = 4.55e-4; % T magnitude of the induction
phi = 65.5; % deg angle between velocity and induction
Solution
First, we will obtain the speed of the electron:
E_k = E_k * e; % J
v_mag = sqrt(2 * E_k / m); % mps
Second, we will resolve the velocity vector into two components, one parallel and the other perpendicular to the induction vector . For simplicity in calculations, I will assume that the induction vector is along the z-axis and has only x and z components.
phi = deg2rad(phi); % rad
b = [0, 0, B];
v = [v_mag * sin(phi), 0, v_mag * cos(phi)];
v_par = dot(v,b) / B * [0 0 1];
v_per = v - v_par;
 
plot_range = -20:2:20;
[X, Y, Z] = meshgrid(plot_range, plot_range, plot_range);
 
U = repmat(b(1), size(X));
V = repmat(b(2), size(X));
W = repmat(b(3), size(X));
 
 
figure;
quiver3(X, Y, Z, U, V, W, 1, 'm', 'LineWidth', 0.5);
 
 
title(['Uniform Induction Field B = [0, 0, ', num2str(B), ']']);
xlabel('X-axis');
ylabel('Y-axis');
zlabel('Z-axis');
axis equal;
grid on;

ylim([-5.44 2.53])

To find pitch of a helix we need to determine period:
r = (m * norm(v_per)) / (e * B);
T = (2 * pi * m) / (e * B);

The pitch of a helix equals 9.059e-02 [m].
The angular frequency is:
slow_factor = 1.5e-8;
omega = sign(q) * (e * B) / m;
t = linspace(0, 4.5 * T, 2000);
x = slow_factor * r * cos(omega .* t);
y = slow_factor * r * sin(omega .* t);
z = slow_factor * v_par(3) .* t;
